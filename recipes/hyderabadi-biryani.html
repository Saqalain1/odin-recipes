Hyderabadi Biryani

Hyderabadi Mutton Biryani is a rich, aromatic rice dish from Hyderabad, India, traditionally made by layering partially cooked rice over slow-cooked mutton and finishing it with a dum (steam) cooking process to infuse the flavors.

Ingredients

  • Mutton (goat or lamb): 2 pounds (1 kg), cut into 1½-inch pieces, bone-in recommended
  • Thick yogurt (dahi): 1½ cups (360 ml)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped): 1 cup (tightly packed)
  • Fresh mint leaves (chopped): 1 cup (tightly packed)
  • Green chilies (chopped): 5 to 6, depending on heat preference
  • Ginger (chopped): 4-inch piece
  • Garlic (peeled): 8–10 cloves
  • Lime juice: 2 tablespoons (30 ml)
  • Salt: 3 teaspoons (15 ml), or to taste
  • Neutral-flavored oil: ½ cup (120 ml), plus extra for frying onions
  • Crispy fried onions: 1 cup (packed), or 200 g store-bought
  • Biryani masala powder: 4 tablespoons (60 g)
  • Shahi jeera (caraway seeds): 1 teaspoon (5 g)
  • Cloves: 5
  • Cinnamon sticks: 3 (4-inch pieces)
  • Black cardamom: 1 (optional)
  • Green cardamom pods: 6
  • Saffron strands: 10–15, soaked in 120 ml warm milk for 15–20 minutes
  • Aged Basmati rice: 700–750 g (about 3½ cups), soaked for 45 minutes to 1 hour
  • Water: 3 liters (12.5 cups) for rice
  • Ghee: 140 ml (½ cup), for layering
  • Kishmish (sultanas): ½ cup (75 g), fried in ghee
  • Cashews: ½ cup (75 g), fried in ghee
  • Rose extract: A few drops
  • Kewra extract: A few drops
  • ellow food coloring: A few drops (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a blender, combine green chilies, ginger, garlic, coriander leaves, and mint leaves with 3 tablespoons of water. Blend into a coarse paste, scraping the sides as needed.
  2. Wash the mutton thoroughly and drain well. In a large bowl, mix the mutton with yogurt, green paste, fried onions, salt, lime juice, oil, and biryani masala powder. Ensure even coating. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Soak the aged Basmati rice for 45 minutes to 1 hour. Drain completely and set aside.
  4. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil. Add whole spices (cloves, cinnamon, black cardamom, green cardamom, bay leaf, and shahi jeera) and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the soaked rice and stir gently. Add 8 cups of water, salt (slightly more than a good soup), and the remaining spices. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and cook until rice is 80% done (grains should be tender but not mushy)
  5. Drain the rice immediately in a colander and set aside.
  6. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). In the Dutch oven, layer the marinated mutton evenly, starting with larger pieces at the bottom.
  7. Spread half of the cooked rice evenly over the mutton. Sprinkle half of the saffron milk, 1 teaspoon garam masala, fried kishmish, cashews, and chopped coriander and mint leaves over the rice.
  8. Layer the remaining rice on top. Repeat the seasoning: saffron milk, garam masala, herbs, and fried onions.
  9. Cover the pot tightly with aluminum foil, then seal the lid. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45 minutes, then reduce temperature to 300°F (150°C) and bake for another 50–60 minutes.
  10. Turn off the oven and let the biryani rest for 15 minutes before opening the lid.
  11. Gently mix the rice and mutton together to combine flavors without breaking the grains.
