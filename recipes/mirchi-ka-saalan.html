Mirchi Ka Saalan

Hyderabadi Mirchi Ka Salan is a rich, tangy, and aromatic curry from Hyderabad, traditionally served with biryani. The key to its unique flavor lies in the roasted peanut-sesame-coconut paste and slow-cooked tamarind-based gravy, which gives it a balanced spicy, sour, and nutty taste.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup raw peanuts
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds
  • ¼ cup desiccated or dry coconut (copra)
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds (khuskhus) optional
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 7–8 dried red chilies (optional, for depth)
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 inch ginger, chopped
  • 4–5 garlic cloves
  • Water, as needed

Step-by-step instructions

  1. Dry roast peanuts, sesame seeds, coconut, poppy seeds, cumin, coriander seeds, and red chilies (if using) in a pan over medium heat until golden and aromatic. Cool completely.
  2. Grind the roasted ingredients into a smooth paste with ginger, garlic, and a little water. Set aside.
  3. Wash and dry green chilies. Slit them lengthwise, deseed carefully, and keep stems intact.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the chilies on medium-low heat until blistered and soft. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  5. In the same oil, add mustard seeds, cumin, nigella, fenugreek (if using), and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
  6. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes.
  7. Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook for 1 minute.
  8. Add the ground masala paste and cook on low heat, stirring continuously, for 8–10 minutes until the oil begins to separate.
  9. Add tamarind pulp, chopped tomato (if using), salt, jaggery, and 1½–2 cups water. Mix well and simmer for 15–20 minutes until the gravy thickens and oil floats on top.
  10. Gently add the fried chilies and simmer for another 5–10 minutes so they absorb the flavors.
  11. Turn off the heat. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot with Hyderabadi biryani, plain rice, or roti.
